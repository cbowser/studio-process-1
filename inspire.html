design process

Reas' design process involves determining a starting point, making numerous iterations, and continously exploring the behaviors made through code. For instance, to start the process, Reas will set one strict rule to follow while coding. He will then expand from this starting point, flowing through iterations. This intuitive process results in unique and unexpected outcomes.

The image to the right provides an example of Reas' process. Pictured is a still image of the elements. The forms are the circles and lines. The behaviors for this example were: move in a straight line, constrain to surface, change direction while touching another element.

This is another example of the elements. The line is the form used and the behaviors include move in a straight line, enter from the opposite edge after moving off the surface, orient toward the direction of an element that is touching, deviate from the current direction.
